# Master Services and SaaS Agreement (Template)

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

This Master Services and SaaS Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

- **Provider:** [YOUR LEGAL ENTITY], a company organized under the laws of [JURISDICTION], with registered address at [ADDRESS] ("Provider").
- **Client:** [CLIENT LEGAL ENTITY], a company organized under the laws of [JURISDICTION], with registered address at [ADDRESS] ("Client").

Provider and Client may each be referred to as a "Party" and together as the "Parties."

## 1. Purpose and Scope
Provider will supply Client with access to Provider's hosted point-of-sale and business management platform, including implementation and related professional services, as described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW").

## 2. Definitions
- **"Platform"** means Provider's PrimePOS software platform, including related modules, updates, and documentation.
- **"Services"** means implementation, onboarding, configuration, support, maintenance, and training as detailed in each SOW.
- **"Client Data"** means data submitted by or on behalf of Client to the Platform.
- **"Order"** means any signed quote, proposal, order form, or SOW that references this Agreement.
- **"Confidential Information"** means non-public business, technical, legal, and financial information disclosed by one Party to the other.

## 3. Subscription and License Grant
Subject to this Agreement and payment of all applicable fees, Provider grants Client a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable right during the Term to access and use the Platform for Client's internal business operations.

## 4. Restrictions
Client shall not, and shall not permit any third party to:
- Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Platform.
- Copy, modify, distribute, or create derivative works of the Platform except as expressly permitted.
- Resell, rent, lease, sublicense, or provide unauthorized third-party access.
- Use the Platform in violation of any law or third-party right.

## 5. Term and Renewal
- **Initial Term:** [12] months beginning on the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier under this Agreement.
- **Renewal:** Automatically renews for successive [12]-month terms unless either Party gives written notice at least [30/60] days before the end of the then-current term.

## 6. Fees, Invoicing, and Payment
- Client shall pay fees specified in each Order.
- Invoices are due within [15/30] days of invoice date.
- Overdue amounts may accrue interest at [1.5% per month] or the maximum lawful rate, whichever is lower.
- Fees are exclusive of taxes. Client is responsible for all applicable taxes except taxes based on Provider's net income.

## 7. Implementation and Client Dependencies
Provider will perform implementation in accordance with the applicable SOW. Client agrees to provide timely access to required personnel, systems, data, approvals, test environments, hardware, and networking.

Any delay caused by Client may reasonably extend project timelines and may affect fees where additional effort is required.

## 8. Acceptance
Unless otherwise stated in an SOW:
- Deliverables are deemed accepted if Client does not provide written notice of material non-conformity within [10] business days of delivery.
- If rejected with sufficient detail, Provider will use reasonable efforts to correct material non-conformities and re-submit the deliverable.

## 9. Support and Service Levels
Provider shall provide support in accordance with **Schedule A (Service Level Agreement)** attached to this Agreement.

## 10. Data Ownership and Processing
- Client retains all rights, title, and interest in Client Data.
- Client grants Provider a limited right to host, copy, transmit, process, and display Client Data solely as required to provide the Services.
- Provider may generate anonymized and aggregated analytics that do not identify Client or personal data.

## 11. Security
Provider will maintain commercially reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards, including but not limited to encryption in transit, access controls, backup procedures, and vulnerability management.

## 12. Confidentiality
Each Party shall:
- Use the other Party's Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement.
- Protect Confidential Information with at least reasonable care.
- Not disclose Confidential Information except to personnel and contractors with a need to know and equivalent confidentiality obligations.

Confidentiality obligations survive for [3-5] years after termination, except trade secrets which survive as long as protected by law.

## 13. Compliance Responsibilities
Client is responsible for lawful use of the Platform, including compliance with local tax, accounting, labor, consumer, privacy, and point-of-sale regulations applicable to Client's operations.

Where card payments are integrated, each Party is responsible for its own obligations under applicable payment processor agreements and PCI-related responsibilities allocated in the relevant SOW.

## 14. Warranties and Disclaimers
- Provider warrants that Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner.
- Provider does not warrant uninterrupted or error-free operation of the Platform.
- Except as expressly stated in this Agreement, the Platform and Services are provided "as is" and all other warranties are disclaimed to the fullest extent permitted by law.

## 15. Indemnification
### 15.1 By Provider
Provider shall defend and indemnify Client against third-party claims alleging the Platform infringes intellectual property rights, except to the extent arising from:
- Client modifications;
- unauthorized use;
- use in combination with non-Provider products not supplied or approved by Provider;
- Client Data.

### 15.2 By Client
Client shall defend and indemnify Provider against third-party claims arising from Client Data, Client's unlawful use of the Platform, or Client's breach of this Agreement.

## 16. Limitation of Liability
To the fullest extent permitted by law:
- Neither Party shall be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages, or any loss of profits, revenues, goodwill, or business interruption.
- Each Party's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable by Client to Provider in the [12] months preceding the event giving rise to liability.
- The liability cap does not apply to obligations that cannot be limited by law, fraud, willful misconduct, or each Party's indemnity obligations (if required by local law).

## 17. Suspension
Provider may suspend access to the Platform immediately if reasonably necessary to prevent security incidents, illegal activity, or material harm. For payment default, Provider will provide written notice and a cure period of [7-14] days where commercially practicable.

## 18. Termination
- Either Party may terminate for material breach not cured within [30] days of written notice.
- Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party becomes insolvent, enters liquidation, or ceases business operations.
- On termination, Client must pay all undisputed accrued fees due up to termination date.

## 19. Exit and Data Return
Upon written request within [30/60] days after termination, Provider will make available a reasonable export of Client Data in a commonly readable format, subject to payment of outstanding undisputed fees and applicable legal retention obligations.

## 20. Non-Solicitation (Optional)
During the Term and for [12] months thereafter, neither Party shall knowingly solicit for employment key personnel of the other Party directly involved in delivery under this Agreement, except via general public recruitment not directed at such personnel.

## 21. Force Majeure
Neither Party shall be liable for delay or failure to perform caused by events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, acts of government, war, terrorism, labor disputes, internet outages, or utility failures.

## 22. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution
This Agreement is governed by the laws of [JURISDICTION], excluding conflict-of-law rules.

Any dispute not resolved through good-faith negotiation within [30] days shall be resolved by [courts of competent jurisdiction in VENUE / binding arbitration under RULES in VENUE].

## 23. Notices
Legal notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by courier, registered mail, or email to the notice addresses designated below (or updated by written notice).

## 24. Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except to an affiliate or in connection with merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, provided the assignee assumes all obligations.

## 25. Entire Agreement; Order of Precedence
This Agreement, including all SOWs, schedules, and addenda, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior discussions.

Order of precedence in case of conflict:
1. Signed Order/SOW terms specific to the project.
2. Data Processing Addendum (where required by law).
3. Service Level Agreement.
4. This Agreement.

## 26. Counterparts and Electronic Signature
This Agreement may be signed in counterparts and via electronic signature, each of which is deemed an original and all of which together form one instrument.

## Schedule A - Service Level Agreement (SLA)

### A1. Availability Target
- Monthly uptime target: **99.9%** excluding planned maintenance and force majeure.

### A2. Planned Maintenance
- Planned maintenance windows: [DAY/TIMEZONE], with at least [48 hours] notice unless emergency action is required.

### A3. Incident Severity and Response Times
- **Severity 1 (Critical):** Complete outage or transaction-blocking issue. Response within [30 minutes]; workaround target [4 hours]; resolution target [8 hours].
- **Severity 2 (High):** Major functionality degradation. Response within [2 hours]; resolution target [2 business days].
- **Severity 3 (Normal):** Minor issue or non-blocking defect. Response within [1 business day]; resolution in a scheduled release.

### A4. Service Credits (Optional)
If Provider fails to meet monthly uptime target, Client may request service credits as follows:
- Uptime below 99.9% and above 99.0%: [5%] of monthly subscription fee.
- Uptime 99.0% or below: [10%] of monthly subscription fee.

Total credits in any month are capped at [20%] of monthly subscription fee.
